Second Kinematic
Equation

The second equation of the equations of motion with a constant acceleration is d = vi t + 1/2 a t 2 , which is an equation that calculates the distance traveled by the body using the initial speed Vi, time t, and acceleration a.

To calculate the initial speed from the equation of second motion, you must have the distance d, acceleration a, and time t. Then we subtract the distance from the product of multiplying half the acceleration by the time squared and divide them by the time....

Vi = d - 1/2a * t2 / t
